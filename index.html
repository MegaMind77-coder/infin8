Rules

General Rules

  • Judges' decisions are final.
  • Contests pick chits that have topics.
  • No trading topics.
  • No repetition of ideas and prolonged gaps.
  • A topic can be interpreted in any relevant way.
  • Timer stops at 60s.
  • Judging criteria is – fluency of thought, non-repetition of ideas, relevance to topic and body language (all out of 5).
  • No offensive/abusive language, points will be deduced for the same.
  • Top 6 contestants of the first round will go into the second round.
  • Top 2 of the second will go into a final round.

Final Round Rules

  • Single two minute round.
  • The highest scorer from the previous round will start.
  • The opponent must look for errors in grammar, content mistakes, hesitancy and repition and stop the speaker by shouting out JAM. The judges will validate the objection. If valid, the opponent gets a point and can start speaking, else the initial speaker gets a point and continues.
  • More points to the speakers who holds on to the mike for a longer time.

General Rules

  • Contestants are given a fixed amount of money at the beginning of the game. They will not receive any money after this.
  • The game is split into rounds. In each round a fixed number of items are auctioned.
    • The contestants will write down their bids. The bid values are not disclosed to other participants.
    • The highest bidder will win the item at the price of the second highest bidder.
    • If someone is forced to pay more money than they currently possess, they are out of the game.
  • The game's objective is to maximize one’s total value. The value a contestant has is the sum of value of the money he/she possesses plus that of the items he has.
  • At the instant an item is bought it has a certain fixed value. This price does not change from item to item.
  • At the end of each round, the value of the money a contestant has decreases by 10%. The initial value of some amount of money is the same as the amount.
  • The value of a contestant’s goods increases by 15% at the end of each round.
  • At the end of all the rounds , the contestant with the highest value wins.
